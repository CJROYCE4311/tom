Discussion Summary & Options

1. GeneSight Testing (Genetic Analysis)

What it is: A cheek swab test that analyzes DNA to see how the body processes psychiatric medications.

  • Benefit: Reduces "trial and error." Tells the doctor which meds work best for Tom's body.
  • Cost: Typically free for Medicaid/Medicare. Commercial insurance: 98% pay $330 or less. Financial assistance is available.

2. Medication Plan

Cobenfy (Xanomeline/Trospium) For Voices/Hallucinations

The Goal: To stop the auditory hallucinations.

  • Advantage: Targets different receptors than older drugs. No weight gain or sleepiness (crucial for Hy-Vee job).
  • Speed: Often works fast (2-3 days).
  • Risks: Nausea, indigestion, high blood pressure.
Lybalvi (Olanzapine + Samidorphan) For Mood & Weight Control

The Goal: Replace current Olanzapine with a version that stops weight gain.

  • Advantage: Samidorphan helps block the weight gain side effect of Olanzapine.
  • CRITICAL WARNING: DO NOT take with opioids. Blocks opioid receptors; causes severe withdrawal.
  • Status: Waiting on insurance approval (Prior Authorization).
Current Olanzapine (Zyprexa) Energy Management

The Change: Lowering dose from 20mg to 15mg.

  • Why: Reduce sedation/tiredness for the new job.
