### Framing the "Failed Work Attempt" Asset
- **Prior jobs as UWAs:** Any employment lasting under 6 months, followed by a break due to psychiatric crisis, homelessness, or inability to function, qualifies as an Unsuccessful Work Attempt (UWA). UWAs do not count as SGA and do not defeat a disability claim. The pattern of two or more UWAs separated by crises is a primary clinical indicator of schizophrenia's impact on vocational stability.
- **Current Hy-Vee job:** Building toward a second successive UWA if it ends within 6 months (started Feb 9, 2026 — clock is running). Document everything: attendance issues, accommodation needs, supervisor feedback, any days missed due to symptoms.
- **The 6-month rule:** If Tom leaves Hy-Vee before August 9, 2026, the entire employment episode can be classified as a UWA. If he makes it past 6 months, SSA will scrutinize it more carefully but the Trial Work Period (TWP) protections kick in.

### The Solution: MIWD (Medicaid Insurance for Workers with Disabilities)
Nebraska's MIWD program is specifically designed for working disabled adults. Tom fits it.

| Criterion | Tom | MIWD Requirement | Match |
|---|---|---|---|
| Age | 26 | 16–64 | Yes |
| Disability | Schizophrenia | SSA determination OR Nebraska SRT | Pending (see below) |
| Earned income | ~$1,300–$1,558/mo | Must have earned income | Yes |
| Income level | ~85–102% FPL | 101–250% FPL | Marginal at low end |
| Premium | — | $0 below 200% FPL (~$3,130/mo) | Would owe $0 |
| Asset limit | Likely low | Under $4,000 individual | Likely yes |

MIWD coverage is identical to Heritage Health Adult for behavioral health — full psychiatry, therapy, medications, hospitalization.

### The Disability Determination — SRT Pathway
MIWD requires a formal disability determination. Tom does not need to wait for SSA to approve his SSDI claim. Nebraska DHHS has its own **State Review Team (SRT)** that uses SSA's criteria to make determinations independently.

**SRT Process:**
1. Call DHHS (1-855-632-7633) and ask to apply for MIWD and request an SRT disability determination
2. Submit medical documentation — Tom's psychiatrist's records (diagnosis, medication history, functional limitations) should be sufficient
3. SRT reviews and makes a determination — this is faster than waiting for SSA

**Recommended sequence:**
1. Log into iServe (iserve.nebraska.gov, tomburbach99 / LNERockets99!) — confirm which Medicaid category Tom is currently in
2. Contact DHHS to initiate SRT determination and MIWD application
3. Once SRT approves → transfer to MIWD → income is no longer a disqualifying factor

### The Fallback: If the Job Ends While He's in MIWD

### Coordination of Benefits: How the Two Insurance Cards Work Together
Nebraska Medicaid is the **payer of last resort** by federal law.
- **UHC is always billed first** — UHC pays its portion, generates an EOB
- **Medicaid is billed second** — covers remaining copays/deductibles/cost-sharing up to the Medicaid allowable rate
- Tom's out-of-pocket cost on covered services should be near **$0**
- He must present **both cards** at every appointment and tell the desk: "UHC is primary, Medicaid is secondary"
- Never pay a copay at the window without first verifying whether Medicaid covers it
