## Chronological History

### 2020: Accident, Diagnosis, Early Instability

**2020-02-07 (approx) — Psychotic break and accident**
Tom has a severe episode of psychosis while driving, crashes his car into two other vehicles, and is admitted to a psychiatric ward. He is diagnosed with "drug-induced schizophrenia" (later revised to primary schizophrenia). His right forearm is badly injured — tendons, arteries, and nerves severed. Surgery is successful but leaves permanent fine motor deficits in his dominant hand. The accident may have been a suicide attempt.

**2020 — Pre- and post-accident behavior**
Tom has prior episodes of paranoia and property destruction (e.g., throwing his phone out of a car window). After the accident he continues to show impaired judgment.

**2020 — First post-accident living attempts**
Tom rotates between living with Miki and Chris in Colorado Springs, with Kent, with Jean, and with Jimmy. Each household sees the same patterns: minimal engagement with treatment, blaming others, quitting or losing jobs, refusing to own his illness.

**2020 — Brief stay with Miki and Chris**
Tom lives briefly with Miki and Chris. After they present reasonable house rules, he refuses to follow them and persuades Jean to let him live with her instead.

**2020-02 — Initial SSI application**
Miki completes an initial online Social Security disability application for Tom. The application remains incomplete for years.

### Late 2021: Colorado Lease and Car

**Late 2021 — Tom returns to Miki and Chris in Colorado**
Tom insists he will not abide by house rules and resists expectations around treatment and work.

**Late 2021 — Miki signs a lease for Tom**
Seven-month lease at approximately $2,000/month plus utilities so Tom can get settled, get a job, and live independently near Miki and Chris.

**Late 2021 — Car purchase**

**Late 2021 — Amazon job failure**

**Late 2021 or early 2022 — Tom runs to Jimmy**
When Miki and Chris confront Tom about his inactivity and set expectations, he rejects the boundaries and runs to live with Jimmy instead.

Over several years after the accident, Tom cycles between Miki/Chris, Kent, Jean, and Jimmy. Each host reports the same themes: takes meds inconsistently, barely participates in therapy, blames others, quits jobs or gets fired.
