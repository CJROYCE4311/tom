# Tom Burbach — Background Summary for Therapist
*Prepared February 26, 2026 by Miki and Chris Royce*

---

Thomas Andrew Burbach (DOB: April 28, 1999) is a 26-year-old man diagnosed with schizophrenia. He is the son of Michaeleane Royce (formerly Burbach) and Kent Burbach, who divorced when Tom was young. His older brother is James (Jimmy) Burbach.

## The Onset (2020)

In February 2020, Tom experienced a psychotic episode, crashed his car into two other vehicles, and was admitted to a psychiatric ward. His right forearm sustained severe injuries requiring surgery, with residual fine motor deficits. The accident may have been a suicide attempt. He was initially diagnosed with "drug-induced schizophrenia," though the family has long believed the underlying condition is primary schizophrenia. In the years that followed, Tom cycled between family households — Miki and Chris in Colorado, his father Kent, his grandmother Jean, and his brother Jimmy — with consistent patterns: inconsistent medication, minimal therapy engagement, job losses, and externalizing blame.

## Treatment History (2023–2025)

In November 2023, after years of cycling between family homes, Miki and Jean arranged residential treatment at We Level Up in Florida. Tom was transferred in December 2023 to The Edge/Forge in Anaheim, California for long-term residential care. Over nearly a year there, he made genuine progress and held a part-time job at Vons grocery store. In November 2024, he left The Forge and rented a room from a man named Roberto near Anaheim, continuing to work at Vons.

In January 2025, Tom stopped all medications, quit his job at Vons, and walked away from his housing. He was homeless and unreachable for approximately three weeks until he was found on February 2, 2025. He was placed at several crisis facilities in California but fled at least four of them before stabilizing at Axis Behavioral Health in Palm Springs. His therapist there, Kat, confirmed that Tom externalizes blame and does not truly engage in treatment; she recommended the gray rock method for family interactions. After Axis, Tom returned to The Forge in fall 2025. In October 2025, Jean flew him to her home, and he eventually moved to Lincoln, Nebraska to live with his brother Jimmy.

## Current Situation (Early 2026)

Tom is now living in Lincoln, Nebraska with Jimmy. He began working at Hy-Vee grocery on February 9, 2026, earning $15/hour at 20–24 hours/week — approximately $1,300–$1,558/month gross. This is a meaningful positive step.

He is receiving outpatient psychiatric care at CenterPointe in Lincoln and his medication regimen is being actively adjusted: Olanzapine reduced from 20mg to 15mg to address sedation, Cobenfy (xanomeline/trospium) added to target persistent hallucinations, and Asenapine recently added (effects not yet established). Nebraska Medicaid was approved effective February 1, 2026.

His Social Security disability application (originally filed 2020, CA DDS Case ID 16321562) remains pending. The SSA-3380 Third Party Function Report was completed and submitted in March 2026 along with a personal statement from Miki and a Section E addendum. The Nebraska SS office confirmed in March 2026 that Tom's income is below SGA. A March 20, 2026 deadline to provide NE Medicaid with insurance verification is pending.

## Family Dynamics

Miki has been Tom's primary advocate and financial supporter throughout. She has spent significant resources on treatment, housing, insurance (including ~$3,000 in COBRA premiums), and logistics. She is now firmly repositioning as his mother rather than his case manager, with continued support contingent on medication compliance and genuine effort. Her mother Jean has contributed financially but is in poor health and has historically enabled avoidance of accountability out of fear. Chris supports a structured approach: Tom's recovery must be treated as a full-time job, and the family will not sustain open-ended support once Jean is no longer able to provide it.

Despite chronic avoidance, Tom has demonstrated real intelligence and capacity for insight — visible in personal essays he has written and in moments of genuine engagement when adequately supported and medicated.
