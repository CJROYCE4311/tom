# Tom Burbach — Background Summary for Therapist
*Prepared February 26, 2026 by Miki and Chris Royce*

---

Thomas Andrew Burbach (DOB: April 28, 1999) is a 26-year-old man diagnosed with schizophrenia. He is the son of Michaeleane Royce (formerly Burbach) and Kent Burbach, who divorced when Tom was young. His older brother is James (Jimmy) Burbach.

## The Onset (2020)

In February 2020, Tom experienced a psychotic episode, crashed his car into two other vehicles, and was admitted to a psychiatric ward. His right forearm sustained severe injuries requiring surgery, with residual fine motor deficits. The accident may have been a suicide attempt. He was initially diagnosed with "drug-induced schizophrenia," though the family has long believed the underlying condition is primary schizophrenia. In the years that followed, Tom cycled between family households — Miki and Chris in Colorado, his father Kent, his grandmother Jean, and his brother Jimmy — with consistent patterns: inconsistent medication, minimal therapy engagement, job losses, and externalizing blame.

## Treatment History (2022–2025)

In late 2022, Miki arranged residential treatment at We Level Up in Florida, followed by a transfer to The Edge in California. Tom made incremental progress — eventually taking a grocery store job and securing a room near the facility — but stopped his medication after Christmas 2023, quit his job, and became aggressive with roommates. He refused to return to The Edge.

From December 2023 through fall 2024, Tom completed a program in Santa Ana, California (inpatient, partial hospitalization, and intensive outpatient levels of care). He was discharged to independent living with part-time grocery work, but by late 2024 had again quit his job, left his housing, and stopped medication — spending more than three weeks homeless.

In early 2025, Tom was admitted to Axis treatment center in Palm Springs after fleeing multiple crisis placements. His therapist there, Kat, confirmed that Tom externalizes blame and does not truly engage in treatment; she recommended the gray rock method for family interactions. He returned briefly to The Edge/Forge before moving to Nebraska in October 2025 to live with his brother Jimmy.

## Current Situation (Early 2026)

Tom is now living in Lincoln, Nebraska with Jimmy. He began working at Hy-Vee grocery on February 9, 2026, earning $15/hour at 30 hours/week — approximately $1,950/month gross. This is a meaningful positive step.

He is engaged with a psychiatrist and his medication regimen is being actively adjusted: reducing olanzapine to address sedation, adding Cobenfy (xanomeline/trospium) to target persistent hallucinations, and pursuing Lybalvi for weight management. Nebraska Medicaid was approved effective February 1, 2026.

Two critical deadlines are active: a March 1 deadline to respond to California DDS regarding his long-pending Social Security disability application, and a March 20 deadline to provide Nebraska Medicaid with insurance verification. Tom's Hy-Vee income exceeds the 2026 SGA threshold, which creates a complication for his disability claim that must be actively managed.

## Family Dynamics

Miki has been Tom's primary advocate and financial supporter throughout. She has spent significant resources on treatment, housing, insurance (including ~$3,000 in COBRA premiums), and logistics. She is now firmly repositioning as his mother rather than his case manager, with continued support contingent on medication compliance and genuine effort. Her mother Jean has contributed financially but is in poor health and has historically enabled avoidance of accountability out of fear. Chris supports a structured approach: Tom's recovery must be treated as a full-time job, and the family will not sustain open-ended support once Jean is no longer able to provide it.

Despite chronic avoidance, Tom has demonstrated real intelligence and capacity for insight — visible in personal essays he has written and in moments of genuine engagement when adequately supported and medicated.
