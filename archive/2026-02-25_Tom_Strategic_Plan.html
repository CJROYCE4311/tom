Social Security — Protecting the Disability Claim

The "Failed Work Attempt" Asset

Tom's history of short employment followed by crisis is not a mark against him with Social Security — it is one of the strongest forms of evidence that he cannot sustain work. This must be framed and documented correctly.

The Legal Tool: Unsuccessful Work Attempt (UWA) Any job that ends within 6 months due to a disabling condition does not count as Substantial Gainful Activity. This means it cannot be used to deny the disability claim. If the Hy-Vee job ends before August 9, 2026, Tom's attorney or advocate files Form SSA-821 to formally classify it as a UWA.

Nebraska Medicaid — The Critical Income Problem

Tom Is Currently in the Wrong Medicaid Category Tom was approved under Heritage Health Adult (ACA expansion), which has an income limit of ~$1,733/month. At 20–24 hrs/wk (~$1,300–$1,558/month), he is within this limit. MIWD remains a worthwhile goal to future-proof coverage if hours increase, but the immediate Medicaid termination risk is resolved at current income.

The Solution: Transfer to MIWD

Nebraska's MIWD (Medicaid Insurance for Workers with Disabilities) program is specifically designed for working disabled adults. Tom's income, disability, and employment all fit it exactly — and he would owe zero monthly premium.

Getting the Disability Determination Without Waiting for SSA

Tom doesn't have to wait for his SSDI application to be approved. Nebraska DHHS has its own State Review Team (SRT) that makes disability determinations using SSA's own criteria, independently and faster.

SRT Process
  1. Call DHHS at 1-855-632-7633 — ask to apply for MIWD and request an SRT disability determination
  2. Submit Tom's psychiatric records (diagnosis, medication history, functional limitations)
  3. SRT reviews and approves → Tom transfers to MIWD → income is no longer a disqualifying factor
Critical Warning Do not voluntarily report Tom's income to DHHS and trigger a Heritage Health termination before the MIWD transfer is in place. Complete the MIWD application first, then the income is covered under the correct category.

The Fallback Scenario — If Tom Loses the Job

This Is the Whole Point of This Plan Tom may not be able to hold this job. Schizophrenia, weight gain from medications, morning sedation, and the cycle of his illness all create real risk. The plan below is designed so that if the job ends, the benefit safety net is already in place — not something to scramble for afterward.

Scenario A: Job ends before August 9, 2026 (before 6-month mark)

  • SSA: File Form SSA-821 within 30 days. Classify Hy-Vee as an Unsuccessful Work Attempt. The employment will not count against the disability claim. The claim actually gets stronger — two UWAs separated by a psychiatric crisis is a primary indicator of schizophrenia's vocational impact.
  • Medicaid: If in MIWD — report job loss to DHHS within 30 days via iServe. MIWD requires earned income; Tom transfers back to Heritage Health Adult (expansion) since his income returns to $0. Coverage continues without a gap if reported promptly.
  • Income: Tom will need support from Jean, Jimmy, or other sources until SSI/SSDI is approved.

Scenario B: Job ends after August 9, 2026 (6+ months)

  • SSA: SSA will scrutinize this more closely — 6+ months of SGA-level earnings can be harder to frame as a UWA. However, if Tom's earnings were kept below SGA using IRWEs, or if SSA had already approved his claim, this is manageable.
  • Trial Work Period (TWP): If SSDI is approved while he's working, he enters the TWP — 9 months in which he can earn any amount and still receive SSDI benefits. After TWP, the Extended Period of Eligibility (EPE) allows SSDI to resume in months he earns below SGA.
  • Medicaid: Same as Scenario A — report loss, transfer back to Heritage Health Adult.

Scenario C: SSDI is approved while Tom is still working

  • Tom enters the 9-month Trial Work Period — full SSDI benefits continue regardless of income during this period.
  • After TWP: 36-month Extended Period of Eligibility. SSDI payments continue in any month income falls below SGA ($1,690).
  • Medicare eligibility begins 24 months after SSDI start date — eventually supplements or replaces UHC/Haleon coverage.
  • Medicaid may continue (check MIWD eligibility with SSDI income) or Medicare becomes primary.
