Discussion Summary & Options

1. GeneSight Testing (Genetic Analysis)

What it is: A cheek swab test that analyzes your DNA to see how your body processes psychiatric medications.

  • Benefit: Reduces "trial and error." It tells the doctor which meds work best for your body and which ones might cause bad side effects.
  • Cost: Typically free for Medicaid/Medicare. For commercial insurance (like yours), 98% of people pay $330 or less. Financial assistance is available if income is low.

2. Medication Plan

Cobenfy (Xanomeline/Trospium) For Voices/Hallucinations

The Goal: To stop the auditory hallucinations.

  • Why this is good: It targets different receptors than older drugs. It does not usually cause weight gain or sleepiness (great for your new job).
  • Speed: Often works fast (2-3 days).
  • Risks/Side Effects: Nausea, indigestion, or high blood pressure.
Lybalvi (Olanzapine + Samidorphan) For Mood & Weight Control

The Goal: To replace your current Olanzapine with a version that stops weight gain.

  • Why this is good: Olanzapine works well for your mood, but causes weight gain. The added ingredient (Samidorphan) helps block that weight gain side effect.
  • CRITICAL WARNING: DO NOT take with opioids (painkillers). It blocks opioid receptors, which can cause severe withdrawal or prevent pain meds from working.
  • Status: Waiting on insurance approval (Prior Authorization).
Current Olanzapine (Zyprexa) Energy Management

The Change: Lowering dose from 20mg to 15mg.

  • Why: To help you feel less tired/sedated, which is crucial as you start your new job at Hy-Vee next week.
